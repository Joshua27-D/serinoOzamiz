About Ozamiz

Ozamiz City, situated in Misamis Occidental in the northern part of Mindanao, Philippines, is a vibrant and progressive urban center known as the "Gem of Panguil Bay." The city boasts a harmonious blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural richness, making it a unique destination for travelers and a thriving home for its residents. Ozamiz serves as a hub for commerce, education, and governance in the region, offering modern amenities while maintaining its warm, small-town charm. With its strategic location, it provides access to picturesque landscapes, including lush mountain ranges and serene coastal areas, attracting nature enthusiasts and adventurers alike.

Historical background

The history of Ozamiz City is deeply intertwined with its role as a strategic trading and defense hub during the Spanish colonial period. In 1755, the Spaniards constructed Fort Santiago (Cotta Fort) to guard against pirate invasions, marking the city as a place of historical importance. The fort later became a symbol of resilience and faith, with the addition of the Birhen sa Cotta Shrine, a revered religious icon. Ozamiz officially became a city in 1948 and was named in honor of José Ozámiz, a notable Filipino hero and former senator. Over the decades, the city has grown into a dynamic and culturally rich community, balancing its historic legacy with modern progress.

Ozamiz Famous For

Ozamiz City is famous for Fort Santiago (Cotta Fort), a historic Spanish fort that stands as a testament to the city’s colonial past and a popular landmark for visitors. It is also renowned for the Birhen sa Cotta Shrine, a sacred religious site known for its miraculous stories and deep spiritual significance to devotees. Nature lovers are drawn to the Malindang Mountain Range, which offers stunning trekking opportunities and showcases the area’s rich biodiversity. The city comes alive during the Subayan Keg Subanon Festival, a vibrant celebration that highlights the unique culture and traditions of the indigenous Subanon tribe. Situated along the scenic Panguil Bay, the city thrives as a hub for trade and transportation while offering breathtaking coastal views. Additionally, Ozamiz is celebrated for its delicious local delicacies, including fresh seafood and native treats that reflect the region’s rich culinary heritage.
